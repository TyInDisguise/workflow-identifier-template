# Probing Patterns

## Rambling is signal

Spoken or free-form answers wander. Let them finish. The most valuable details — the workaround, the exception, the frustration — usually arrive as asides, not as direct answers. Probe only after they've finished the thought, not mid-sentence.

When an interviewee uses vague language, probe before moving on. Goal: convert generalities into specifics that can be automated against.

## Vagueness triggers and probes

| They say... | Probe with... |
|-------------|---------------|
| "Usually" / "most of the time" | "What happens the other times?" |
| "The spreadsheet" / "the file" | "Which one specifically? Where does it live?" |
| "We just..." / "I just..." | "Walk me through exactly what 'just' looks like." |
| "It depends" | "Depends on what? Can we list the factors?" |
| "Sometimes" | "Roughly what percent of the time?" |
| "The team" / "someone" | "Who specifically? What role?" |
| "If there's a problem" | "What kinds of problems come up? Most common one?" |
| "I check to make sure" | "What exactly are you looking at? What would make you reject it?" |
| "I use judgment" | "What's an example of a tough call you made recently?" |
| "It's pretty straightforward" | "What trips up new people who try it?" |

## When NOT to probe

- User is visibly frustrated or rushing — note the vagueness, move on, come back later.
- The detail is genuinely irrelevant to automation (e.g., exact UI colors).
- You've already probed the same pattern twice — flag it as a gap in the record rather than pressing harder.
