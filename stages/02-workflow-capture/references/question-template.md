# Interview Question Template

**The 19 topics below are a coverage guarantee.** Every section (A-E) must produce an answer to every topic before the section's mirror-and-advance gate. This is what makes records comparable across workflows and keeps audits reliable.

**Pareto applies to depth and order, not coverage.** Skip the literal wording of a question whose answer is already on the table with high confidence. Reorder when the conversation leads naturally elsewhere. Spend more turns on surprising or complex answers, fewer on simple ones. Do not skip topics.

One question at a time. Mirror after each section before advancing.

This is the **depth pass**. Stage 01 handles breadth (short surface-level questions across many workflows to pick one). Here we go deep on the chosen workflow.

## A. Trigger & Context

1. What starts this task? A meeting, an email, a date, a form, a request?
2. Who or what kicks it off?
3. How often — daily? Weekly? A few times a month?
4. Roughly how many do you handle in a typical day or week?

## B. The Actual Work

5. Walk me through it start to finish, as you actually did it the last time.
6. Which systems or tools do you open, and in what order?
7. What information do you look up, copy, or move between places?
8. Where do you usually wait on someone or something?
9. Anything you do here that isn't in the "official" version?

## C. Decisions

10. Are there points where you have to decide something? Walk me through one.
11. What rule are you following? Try to phrase it as "if X then Y."
12. What's the hardest judgment call in this workflow?
13. What's the most common exception — the thing that breaks the normal path?

## D. Outputs & Handoffs

14. What's the final thing(s) you produce? Document, email, updated record?
15. Who receives it, and what do they do with it next?
16. How do you know the task is done? Does anyone verify?

## E. Pain

17. What part of this is the most frustrating or time-consuming? 
18. If a new hire had to do this tomorrow, what would you warn them about?
19. If you could wave a wand and fix one thing about how this works, what would it be?
