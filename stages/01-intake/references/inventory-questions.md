# Inventory Questions

The **breadth pass.** Short, surface-level questions to surface 5-10
candidate workflows. Keep each candidate shallow — one or two lines.
Don't elicit process detail here; that's stage 02's job.

One question at a time. Mirror after the inventory is built, before
selection.

## A. Business context

Ask first, before surfacing workflows. Keep this tight — 2-3 minutes.

1. What does your company do, in one sentence?
2. What's your role, and who do you work with most?
3. What does a typical week look like for you and your team?

Write a 3-line business summary from these answers. Confirm before
moving on.

## B. Surfacing candidates

Ask open, then narrow. The goal is 5-10 candidates — not fewer, not more.

4. What are the tasks or processes you or your team do *most often*?
5. What are the things that take the most time — even if they're not
   that frequent?
6. What do you find yourself or your team doing manually that feels
   like it could be faster?
7. Is there anything that breaks or creates errors often enough that
   you've thought about fixing it?
8. What do new hires struggle with the most when they join?

These five prompts usually surface 5-10 candidates together. If you have
fewer than 5 after asking them all, probe on the vaguest answer.

## C. Per-candidate capture

For each candidate surfaced, capture these five fields in 1-2 lines
each. Don't go deep.

- **Name** — short descriptive phrase, not a category. "Processing
  vendor invoices" is a name. "Accounting" is not.
- **Frequency** — daily, weekly, a few times a month, a few times a
  year.
- **Performer** — who actually does it (by role, not by name).
- **Interest reason** — why it came up. Is it painful? Slow?
  Error-prone? Just time-consuming?
- **Stability** — is this workflow expected to change significantly in
  the next 6-12 months? (Migration, re-platform, process redesign,
  vendor change.) Yes / No / Unsure.

## D. Before mirroring

Before presenting the inventory back for confirmation:

- Check that every candidate has all five fields.
- If any candidate is vague ("our reporting process"), ask one probe
  to tighten it ("which report specifically?").
- If a candidate's stability answer is "yes" (changing soon), flag it
  in the interest reason — building for a workflow that's about to be
  replaced is usually wasted effort.
- If there are more than 10 candidates, note the 10 that look most
  promising based on `selection-guide.md` criteria — but still show
  the full list to the user for their review.

## Mirroring format

Present the inventory as a short table:

| Workflow | Frequency | Performer | Why it's on the list | Stable? |
|----------|-----------|-----------|----------------------|---------|
| [name] | [freq] | [role] | [interest reason] | [Y/N/?] |

Then ask: "Anything missing, anything we should drop, or anything to
edit?" Update based on their response before advancing to selection.
